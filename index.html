Resources ✦

Everything you need to run a sharp job search — from writing to negotiating to landing the offer.

📝
Resume writing tips
1Lead every bullet with a strong action verb — Led, Built, Reduced, Grew — not Responsible for.
2Quantify impact wherever possible. "Grew pipeline by 40%" beats "helped grow pipeline".
3Keep it to one page if under 8 years experience. Two pages maximum, ever.
4Mirror the exact language from the JD — ATS systems scan for keyword matches.
5Put your most recent and relevant role first. Recruiters spend 7 seconds on a first scan.
🎯
Interview guides
1STAR method — Situation, Task, Action, Result. Use it for every behavioural question.
2Prepare 3 stories that flex across questions — a challenge, a win, a failure.
3Research the company's recent news, product, and competitors before every interview.
4Always have 2–3 thoughtful questions ready for the interviewer — it shows genuine interest.
5Send a follow-up thank you note within 24 hours. Most candidates don't — it stands out.
💰
Salary negotiation
1Never give a number first. Say "I'd love to understand the full package before discussing numbers."
2Research market rates on Glassdoor, Levels.fyi, and LinkedIn Salary before any conversation.
3Negotiate the whole package — base, bonus, equity, PTO, remote flexibility, learning budget.
4Ask for 10–20% above your target. You can always come down; you can't go back up.
5Get the offer in writing before giving notice. Always.
🗺️
Job search strategies
180% of jobs are filled through referrals. Prioritise warm outreach over cold applications.
2Apply within 48 hours of a posting going live — response rates drop sharply after that.
3Set a daily target — 3 quality applications beats 20 spray-and-pray applications every time.
4Build in public — write about your work on LinkedIn. Inbound is the best kind of pipeline.
5Track every application in FitCheck's tracker. Patterns in rejections reveal what to fix.
